  • PANet / Canvas Account

  • How do I change my password?

    To change your password, head to https://mypassword.andover.edu/. Please follow the directions there and provide your current login credentials to change your password. You may be required to set-up password hints.

    At some interval after changing your password, you should be prompted by the email and wireless programs on your devices to enter the new password. Depending on the timing of this, your account may become locked (because the old password is still being used by the devices). If this occurs, please read the locked account section below.

  • I forgot my password. How do I reset it?

    Unfortunately, Techmasters are not able to recover or reset your password for you. To reset your password, you can head over to https://mypassword.andover.edu/ and reset your password by answering your three security questions. If you have forgotten both your password and security answers, you will need to contact the OIT help desk located at the PACC, which is in the basement of the library.

  • It says my account is locked! What do I do?

    If you are confident that you have the correct password but cannot login, it is possible that your account has been locked due to too many invalid login attempts. If that is the case, you can either unlock your account at https://mypassword.andover.edu/ by answering your security questions or wait for the account to unlock automatically after a few hours. You may also choose to contact the OIT help desk located at the PACC, which is in the basement of the library.

  • It says "Failed to Log In" when I try to log into Canvas. What do I do?

    If you are able to log into PAnet, but you cannot log into Canvas, it may be problem with cache/cookies stored in your browser. Please follow the instructions below and then try to log in again:

    If you are using Chrome, please go to Chrome history, select "clear browsing data", choose to obliterate items from "the beginning of time", uncheck every box and then check "cookies", and click "clear browsing data."

    If you are using Firefox, please go to Firefox history, select "Clear Recent History", set the "Time range to clear" to "Everything", uncheck every box and then check "Cookies", and click "Clear Now".

    If you are using Safari on Mac, please click Safari in the upper left corner of your screen. In the menu, click "Preferences". In the window, select the "Privacy" tab. Click "Remove All Website Data", and then select "Remove Now" in the pop up window that appears.

    If you are using another browser, you may follow the browser developer's instructions to remove all cookies.

  • Printers

  • My wireless printer won't work.

    Andover's WiFi runs on the security protocol 802.11x EAP, or WPA2 Enterprise. What you're used to at home (and what wireless printers are usually designed for) is WPA2 Personal. This little difference basically makes your personal at-home printer incompatible with Andover's network. To test if your printer is compatible, you can look up its specifications on the internet or do a quick test: try to connect to Andover's network. If it says an error has occured, your printer does not support WPA2 Enterprise and will not work with the WiFi. If your printer prompts for only a password, your printer likely does not support WPA2 Enterprise. If your printer is designed for Andover's network (slim probability) and gets connected, and you STILL have a problem connecting to it, contact a Techmaster via livechat. Make sure you specify your printer supports the network, because we all immediately assume a student's wireless printer will not work because of the compatibility issues explained thereof.
